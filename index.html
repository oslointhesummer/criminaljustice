The Case for Public Defense Funding in the Bay Area

What are Public Defenders?

"A public defender is a lawyer appointed to represent people who otherwise cannot reasonably afford to hire a lawyer to defend themselves in a trial. The Sixth Amendment of the US Constitution requires the US government to provide free legal counsel to indigent defendants in criminal cases"

Public Defense vs. Law Enforcement Funding in United States

Public defense is funded through a patchwork of state and county resources

In 2012, $2.2 Billion was spent on public defense. Whereas, $155 Billion was spent on policing in 2017

How many cases do public defenders work on a year?

The bar association recommends that public defenders who handle misdemeanor cases do not work more than 400 cases a year and for those who handle felony cases to not work more than 150 cases a year. In addition to state and county funding, Bay Area agencies receive millions of dollars worth of technology through federal grants. Between 2010 and 2020, the Bay Area received $11.9 million worth of technology through federal grants. The most frequently cited terms in federal grants to Bay Area law enforcement relate to surveillance (e.g. "sight," "vision," and "illuminator").

Why Does Surveillance Matter?

Surveillance technologies disproportionately target low-income people and people of color, ultimately leading to increased policing of those communities.

"Cutting-edge technologies are prone to be targeted at communities of color as well. An advocacy group’s deep dive into license plate records from Oakland, Calif., revealed that lower-income minority neighborhoods – regardless of their crime rates – were lined with the devices, while white wealthier neighborhoods could count on having their cars snapped with far less frequency... Another study conducted after a Michigan city installed surveillance cameras in residential neighborhoods found that African-American residents were twice as likely to be surveilled as their white neighbors."

Effects on Racial Make Up of Jail Population
